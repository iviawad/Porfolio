About Me

I am Malek, and I grew up in Jordan. and in life, both good and bad experiences tend to shape the way we build the passion needed to pursue a desired career path.
Unfortunately, cancer is a topic that is close to the hearts of us all. During my second year at University as a Computer Science major,
my Mother was diagnosed with a terminal form of cancer. She struggled with it for two years until she passed away a few months after my graduation.
This experience had a huge impact on me, and was the driving factor in my decision to work in the radiation therapy industry at ViewRay as a Software QA Engineer

As part of the R&D team, I am constantly exposed to the forefront of innovation in the entire field of radiation therapy.
Currently, industry leaders are competing to integrate Artificial Intelligence into their clinical treatment workflows.
This development has the potential to reduce treatment time by four-fold, hence increasing patient throughput by four times while maximizing accuracy and reducing radiation toxicity.

My first practical experience with AI came from my internship with Rababa, one of the top game development companies across the Middle East.
I was responsible for utilizing the Unity Engine in collaboration with C# to create an algorithm for the motion of non-player characters (NPCs).
Immediately, I was fascinated by the ability to recreate the human thought process into a practical, digital form. This positive experience carved my passion to learn and explore diffrent codding languages

I have been utilizing my Software and Technical skills to help the quest to re-envision radiation therapy to conquer cancer.
I support my teammates fully by conducting thorough and detailed work, including designing test plans, scenarios,
scripts, automating solutions or procedures, and taking the initiative to solve technical problems
